--- layout: post title: Death to the .user! date: '2012-09-07T16:11:00.000+01:00' author: SÃ©rgio Agostinho tags: - TFS - Visual Studio - git modified_time: '2014-05-21T15:33:25.826+01:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-6105644668642718046.post-2704747279097261890 blogger_orig_url: http://sergioagostinho-dev.blogspot.com/2012/09/death-to-user.html --- A couple months ago, one of my VS solutions would just crash with a specific project. After a couple of hours, I've found the solution: close Visual Studio, delete all the "*.csproj.user" files, and reload the solution. My theory is that this was caused by TFS merges, since this solution had a few dozen projects and was frequently updated by various users, which in turn caused frequent conflicts. It's no wonder that these files are listed in .gitignore.
